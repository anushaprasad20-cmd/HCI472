Hierarchy 03 · Student's Choice · Experimental

Hybrid futures.

Typefaces Bricolage Grotesque
Instrument Serif
JetBrains Mono

Scale 1.500 · Perfect Fifth

Base 16 px

Three typefaces in conversation — a variable grotesque for weight and structure, an italic display serif for breath and ornament, and a monospace for the voice of the machine. The experiment: can a hierarchy built from three temperaments still feel like one document? This page is the answer.

◆ Specimens · 01 → 07 ◆
Display · H1 81 px · 1.5⁴
Bricolage 700 + Instrument italic accent

A grotesque holds the weight.

H1 belongs to Bricolage Grotesque at 700 — wide optical size, tight tracking, a contemporary voice built for display. A single word steps out into Instrument Serif italic to establish the pattern that will play across this entire page: the grotesque is the structure, the serif is the commentary.

Section · H2 54 px · 1.5³
Instrument Serif Italic 400 · the breath

The serif speaks in italics.

H2 hands the microphone to Instrument Serif. Its thin strokes and elongated forms feel almost hand-drawn at this scale — deliberately ornamental, deliberately unlike the grotesque above. The contrast is the entire design argument: two voices, not blended but alternating.

Subsection · H3 36 px · 1.5²
Bricolage 600 opsz 48 — text-ish

Return to the grotesque, dialed in.

H3 comes back to Bricolage — this time at a smaller optical size, where its proportions behave more like text than display. The return creates rhythm: grotesque, serif, grotesque, serif — a call-and-response that makes the page feel composed rather than collaged.

Minor · H4 24 px · 1.5¹
Instrument Serif Roman 400 · the quiet note

And the serif returns, quietly.

H4 is Instrument Serif in its upright form — no italic, no volume, just the serif's presence as a marker. Small as it is, the typeface switch remains perceptible, which is exactly what a minor heading should be: a signal that a new thought has begun.

Body Text 16 px · 1.5⁰
Bricolage 400 opsz 14 · the workhorse

Body copy stays with Bricolage — a single family doing the long haul, its small-optical-size variant tuned for comfortable reading at sixteen pixels. The grotesque's rounded counters and slightly soft terminals keep the reading surface warm; line-height at 1.65 gives the eye room to breathe. Across long paragraphs, the consistency of the body is what allows every deviation — the serif interjections, the monospace links — to feel purposeful rather than chaotic. Variety is only legible against a steady ground.

Emphasis ~18 px inline
Instrument Serif Italic 400 · color shift

Emphasis is where the serif earns its keep inside running text. Rather than rely on weight or underline, this hierarchy uses a typeface switch — Instrument Serif italic, slightly oversized, tinted terracotta. The shift is immediate and unmistakable. The trick is restraint: one emphasized phrase per paragraph at most, or the contrast collapses into visual noise and the page stops meaning anything at all.

Link Text ~14.4 px inline
JetBrains Mono 500 the machine voice

Links are the boldest move on this page. Rather than re-color body text and call it done, each link is rendered in JetBrains Mono — the typeface of the terminal — inside a tinted sage capsule with a leading arrow glyph. The link visibly is not body text; it is machine-addressable. Try it: open the documentation, or follow the reference. On hover, the capsule fills and the text inverts — a physical feeling of clicking before the click.

Modular Scale

1.500 — the Perfect Fifth.

Large intervals for large contrasts.

Each step is 1.5× the last, starting from a 16 px base. The Perfect Fifth is the most dramatic ratio used across this assignment — intentional here, because three competing typefaces need room to breathe. Large size jumps mean each hierarchy level feels distinctly its own, and the grotesque/serif/mono alternation reads as rhythm rather than confusion. A tighter scale would have made the typeface switches feel accidental. The wider scale earns them.
