02Method

On modular scales.

A modular scale is a sequence of numbers in which each is produced by multiplying the previous by a fixed ratio — the same mathematical logic found in musical intervals. Starting from a 16 px base, a single ratio determines every type size on the page. The result is rhythm: sizes that feel related, not arbitrary. Each hierarchy below uses a different ratio — chosen to match its temperament.

1.333
Perfect Fourth

Used in Editorial Luxe. A classical interval — spacious enough for a display serif to breathe, tight enough to preserve the authority of body text.

1.250
Major Third

Used in Monochromatic System. A gentler ratio suited to a single typeface — differences between levels lean on weight and tracking rather than raw size.

1.500
Perfect Fifth

Used in Hybrid Futures. A dramatic interval — large jumps between levels amplify the contrast between the three competing typefaces.
